Welcome to
München

Where happiness is
always in style

Located at the Isar river, München is the capital city of Bavaria, the second largest state of Germany. However, the cultures and arts that it bears is second to none in this country. Though having been damaged in World War II, the city rises from the ashes and enhanced its beauty with picturesque sceneries and historical buildings. München is the everlasting proof of the Germans' bold characteristics: determined, strong and indestructible.

Aside from its historical and cultural side, München is also known for its being an international center of business, engineering and communication. In fact, München's ability to maintain both its cultural heritage side and its immensely developing technological side has gained the city its reputation as a city of "laptop and lederhosen".

München is home to many world records such as the world's oldest cinema, the world's largest pub or the world’s biggest technology and science museum. Not only that, visitors can also engage in abundant fun and pleasurable activities such as surfing at the Eisbach Canal or going shopping at the Farmers' all-organic market.

Frauenkirche

The Cathedral Church of Our Lady

The Cathedral Church of Our Lady is definitely one of the most, if not the most, recognizable symbols of München. The imagery of the Twin Towers, along with the Town Hall has long been said to define the skyline of this beautiful city.


This gorgeous piece of architecture bears within itself many tourist attractions such as the enormous figure of St. Christopher from 1520 or the three bronze reliefs of Mother Theresa, Rupert Mayer and Kaspar Stanggassinger.

Additionally, the most famous legend here is the Devil’s Footprint (Teufelstritt). Legend has it that this footprint-shape black mark is where the devil stood and made fun of the windowless church. Another version of the legend says that the mark was the furious footprint left by the Devil who was tricked by the church builders. He then rushed outside and turned into the ever howling wind that surrounds Frauenkirche. However the legend is, this Cathedral is a must-come attraction when visiting München.

Nymphenburg Palace

The Cathedral Church of Our Lady

Nymphenburg Palace, a palace that was built out of the love of the parents for their long-awaited newborn, is one of the most famous tourist attractions in München. After being under the possession of five Wittlesback rulers, this magnificent historical and cultural site has changed a lot compared to its original form in 1662.


When coming to the palace, be sure to not miss out on the elegant Nymphenburg garden, the city’s largest city garden. The garden is a harmonious combination between nature and the sophisticated touch of decoration such as many sculptures representing the gods of ancient Greece. It is a fascinating site that is perfect for an experiencing day in München.

Oktoberfest

The world’s largest beer festival

The 2019 Oktoberfest is beginning on September 21st and ends on October 6th. This 204-year-old celebration of Bavarian traditions is one of the main reasons that München welcomes a large number of visitors each year. It is considered one of the world’s largest beer festival. In fact, in 2018, 7.5 millions litres of beer was poured in this one specific occasion.


You can start buying beer from tents from as soon as 10AM to 10:30PM on weekdays and from 9AM to 10:30PM on the weekends. There is also a wine tent (Weinzelt) that serves 15 different types of wine as well as champagne and sparkling wine for those who want to change up their drink. But the main attraction here is the exclusively brewed beer for the festival. The beer price can be quite pricey: starting at €10.70 ($12). So be sure to save up for this one-of-a-kind festival this year.

Get Nude

In the English Garden

For comfort seekers who are looking for a restful and pleasurable afternoon in München, English Garden is the perfect spot to check out. This 3.75 km² wide piece of greenery is considered the lungs of the city and a popular site to go nude at.


In 2013, the city has made it legal for citizens and tourists to come here and strip off their clothes for an afternoon snooze. Even long before the officialization of the practice, nude sunbathing is a popular practice among the Bavarians on sunshiney days in the city.

You can get naked in specific spots in the park: two spots at the southern part along the Isar River, South-western part of Feldmoching Lake, a Northern section of the Englischer Garten and Isar Island Oberfö Hring in the northern part of Isar River. Just be sure to not take out your camera. Though not forbidden, a camera in such a sensitive place can be commonly frowned upon.

Surfing

At the Eisbach Canal

Those who are sports fans or visitors interested in surfing can be assured to find those alike at this attraction. The waves of the Eisbach river at the opening of the English Garden has long been known as a famous spot for river surfing.


The practice of river surfing starts very early in the morning. While the rest of the park is still in silent, you can be sure that at this specific location, several surfers are lining up to surf on the waves of this river. Not until 20 years ago was this practice made legal; however, this site has long been a famous surfing spot since 40 years ago.


Even if you are not a surfer yourself, nowadays, you are welcome to come and watch as the surfers ride on the waves and create outstanding surfing stunts.

The Farmers' Market

If you want to support the local farmers and buy organic products, or simply get lost in an area that offers a wide range of experience, the Viktualienmarkt in München is where you should think of first.


The Victuals Market has 140 stalls and booths that feature abundant products: from exotic flowers and fruits to freshly squeezed juice. Originating from a mere local farmers’ market, Viktualienmarkt raised to become a common meeting point that has hosted abundants events such as brewers’ day, gardeners’ day or a summer festival.


This local site is well known to many tourists for its organic and friendly nature. Even if you are not planning on buying anything from the stalls, a walk in the Viktualienmarkt guarantees a pleasurable experience in München.

Cuisine

Weisswurst

White sausage

Weisswurst, which can be literally translated to ‘White sausage’, is a traditional dish of the Bavarian made from minced veal meat and pork. According to a legend, it was created due to a mistake when one innkeeper ran out of sheep casings and used pork casings in replacement.

Ingredients
Lean veal meat, Lean pork, Pork fat, Dried lemon skin, White pepper, Dried ginger, Freshly grated cardamom, Dried mace, Onion, Parsley, Sausage casings


Place To Try

Apfelstrudel

Apple strudel
